INR MIBOR OIS Market Conventions

The CCIL publishes OIS rates as par swap rates — not zero rates. For ≤1Y tenors, they are simple rates (single payment at maturity). For >1Y tenors, they are quoted as semi-annual coupon equivalent rates (coupons paid every 6 months, bullet principal at maturity). These must be bootstrapped to extract the true zero curve.

Verification: at fixed rate = 6.39892% (CCIL 5Y quote) with bullet principal and semi-annual payments, the solved spread is ~0 bps. This is the market par swap rate by construction.

STEP 1 Short End — Simple Rates (≤1Y)

O/N through 1Y are quoted as simple (linear) rates. Discount factors:

DF(t) = 1 / (1 + r_simple × t) Zero rate (CC): z(t) = −ln(DF(t)) / t
STEP 2 Long End — Bootstrap from Semi-Annual Swap Rates (>1Y)

For each tenor T with quoted semi-annual swap rate S, the par condition is:

(S/2) × Σ DF(t_i) + DF(T) = 1 i=0.5, 1.0, ..., T Where all t_i < T use already-bootstrapped DFs. Solving for the final unknown DF(T): DF(T) = [1 − (S/2) × Σ_{t_i < T} DF(t_i)] / (1 + S/2) Zero rate: z(T) = −ln DF(T) / T

This is done sequentially: 2Y → 3Y → 4Y → 5Y → 7Y → 10Y. Intermediate semi-annual dates (e.g. 2.5Y when bootstrapping 3Y) use log-linear interpolation on already-bootstrapped DFs.

STEP 3 Implied Forward Rates

Once the zero curve is bootstrapped, the annualised simple forward rate for any period [t₁, t₂]:

f_simple(t₁, t₂) = [DF(t₁) / DF(t₂) − 1] / (t₂ − t₁)

This is the rate the OIS market implies for a deposit from t₁ to t₂. Used directly to price each float accrual period.

STEP 4 Float Leg Cash Flows
FloatInterest_i = Outstanding_i × [f_simple(t_{i-1}, t_i) + S/10000] × (days_i / 365)

S is the single constant spread (in bps) solved via bisection. The forward rate varies period by period from the bootstrapped curve.

STEP 5 Solver & Verification
Find S* such that: PV(Float @ f_simple + S*) = PV(Fixed) Bisection: [−500, +1500] bps, 60 iterations, tol = 0.0001 bps

Sanity check: A 5Y bullet IRS at fixed rate = 6.39892% (the CCIL 5Y quoted rate) with semi-annual payments should give S* ≈ 0 bps by construction. This confirms the bootstrapping and forward rate logic are correct.

Loan Schedule Generation
  • Interest frequency: User-specified (semi-annual default, matching IRS convention)
  • Moratorium: No principal repaid; interest accrues on full notional
  • Linear: Equal principal = Notional ÷ number of principal payment dates
  • EMI: Constant instalment at fixed rate; principal = instalment − period interest
  • Bullet: Full notional at maturity only
  • Day count: Actual/365 throughout
